favorite albums of the decade

december 21, 2019

so the decade is coming to an end and there was a lot of great music this decade! music has always been one of my favorite art forms and one of my favorite hobbies is listening to new music and talking about it with friends. these were some of my favorites albums, in no particular order (click the image to expand).

i don't think i could rank these albums but i could elaborate more on my top 5:

Anderson .Paak — Malibu (2016)

Anderson .Paak's charisma and energy is infectious in Malibu. track after track he flexes his incredible vocal range over some warm instrumentals. is blends west coast hip hop — guest appearances include Rapsody, Schoolboy Q, and The Game — as well as r&b and neo-soul. one of the things i especially admire about this album is how expansive its feature list and production credits list are. it's definitely an album that couldn't have come together so well if it wasn't for the work of a lot of people. and that's not to deminish Anderson .Paak's contributions; he's a commanding prescence and his subject matter tends to lean very personal. this album is a consistently feel-good and fun listen.

favorite tracks: "The Season / Carry Me", "Parking Lot", "The Dreamer", "Heart Don't Stand a Chance", "The Bird".

Alvvays — Alvvays (2014)

Alvvays — pronounced "always" — is a canadian indie pop band and their debut self-titled album was one of the best first listens i've ever had to an album. the album takes musical inspiration from subgenres like surf rock, dream pop, and shoegaze which makes for a lush and ethereal sonic pallette. while the production is rather easy-going, the lyrics offer themes of social anxiety and longing. one interesting thing about the lyrics is lead singer Molly Rankin sings them in a very direct manner in that these songs are written addressing someone which makes them even more personal and relatable. this combination makes for a blissful, pensive, and catchy listen.

favorite tracks: "Ones Who Love You", "Party Police", "Dives", "Atop a Cake", "Archie, Marry Me".

Abra — Rose (2015)

Abra is part of atlanta group awful records and while they have a lot of talent on their roster, i think she is my favorite artist on the label. Roses is self-produced album full of spacey and lowkey tracks combining r&b, synthpop, and uk bass. she has a really great voice which goes over really well over the DIY 80s r&b revival and darkwave production. this is a very appealing combination as she is able to incorporate bright vocal samples into her otherwise moody instrumentals. lyrically, Abra writes intimate and vulnarable lyrics filled with metaphors. the last two songs on the album are stripped back remakes of previous songs which include skeletal piano instrumentals and this is where she really showcases her vocal talent.

favorite tracks: "Roses", "Atoms", "Feel", "No Chill", "Human".

Ratking — So it Goes (2014)

i discovered hip hop trio Ratking right as they broke up. such a shame because on So it Goes, they demonstrate such a unique blend of new york hip hop in my opinion. their influences are varied: the album is as grimey as any new york album in the 90s but you can also hear how rapper Wiki takes inspiration from acts like Rage against the Machine and Ol Dirty Bastard while producer Sporting Life looks at both hip hop producers RZA and Madlib as well as indie bands like Animal Collective. not to mention the really sharp lyricism and storytelling by both Wiki and Hak. it's a modern take on classic 90s new york boom bap.

favorite tracks: "*", "Canal", "Protein", "Cocoa '88", "So Sick Stories".

Mach-Hommy — HBO (2016)

Mach-Hommy is a rapper from new jersey making innovative east coast hip hop. he has this off-kilter flow and delivery which when paired with lo-fi production, makes his voice sound grimey and distorted. he's idiosyncratic, often choosing to rap off beat but not too off beat that it's awkard. it took me a bit of time to get into this album but the clever wordplay and lyricism as well as the dusty and abstract instrumentals from producers August Fanon and Daringer make this album very captivating. a special mention to guest appearances from buffalo poet Keisha Plum and buffalo rapper Conway.

favorite tracks: "Ti Geralde", "1080p", "Kapeesh", "Bey Six", "Thank God".
